Cookie Policy

How we use cookies to improve your experience on our website

Last Updated: February 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how SilokiInk LLC ("we," "us," or "our") uses cookies and similar technologies on our website at https://siloki-ink.com. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide useful information to website owners.

Cookies allow websites to:

  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Improve your browsing experience
  • Understand how you interact with the website
  • Enhance website security
  • Analyze website traffic and performance

2. Types of Cookies We Use

SilokiInk LLC uses minimal cookies to ensure the best possible experience while respecting your privacy. We use the following types of cookies:

Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function properly and cannot be disabled. They are typically set in response to actions you take, such as:

  • Session Cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser, used to maintain your session as you navigate our website
  • Security Cookies: Cookies that help protect our website and your information from security risks

These cookies do not collect personally identifiable information and are necessary for basic website functionality.

Analytics Cookies (Google Analytics)

We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors use our website. These cookies collect anonymous information about:

  • Number of visitors to our website
  • Pages visited and time spent on each page
  • How visitors arrived at our website (search engines, direct visits, referrals)
  • General geographic location (country and city level)
  • Device type and browser used
  • User behavior patterns and navigation paths

Important: Google Analytics data is collected anonymously. We use IP anonymization, which means your full IP address is never stored or associated with your data.

3. Why We Use Cookies

We use cookies for several important purposes:

Improve User Experience

Cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website, allowing us to:

  • Identify and fix technical issues
  • Improve website navigation and layout
  • Enhance content based on user interests
  • Optimize page loading times

Understand Website Traffic

Analytics cookies provide valuable insights that help us:

  • Understand which pages are most popular
  • Identify content that resonates with visitors
  • Measure the effectiveness of our services and marketing efforts
  • Make data-driven decisions about website improvements

Maintain Website Security

Essential cookies help us:

  • Protect against fraudulent activity
  • Ensure secure browsing sessions
  • Prevent unauthorized access

4. Google Analytics in Detail

Google Analytics is the primary analytics tool we use to understand website performance and visitor behavior. Here's what you should know:

Anonymous Data Collection

Your privacy is protected: We have enabled IP anonymization in Google Analytics. This means your full IP address is truncated before being stored, making it impossible to identify individual users.

What Google Analytics Tracks

  • Page Views: Which pages you visit on our website
  • Session Duration: How long you spend on our website
  • Bounce Rate: Whether visitors engage with multiple pages or leave after viewing one
  • Traffic Sources: How you found our website (search engines, social media, direct visits)
  • Device Information: Type of device, browser, screen size (no personal device identifiers)
  • Geographic Data: Country and city level location (not precise location)

What Google Analytics Does NOT Track

  • Personally identifiable information (names, email addresses, phone numbers)
  • Precise geolocation data
  • Financial information
  • Passwords or login credentials
  • Sensitive personal data

Google's Privacy Policy

Google Analytics is operated by Google LLC. For more information about how Google uses data, please visit:

  • Google Privacy Policy
  • Google Analytics Data Privacy and Security

5. Third-Party Cookies

The only third-party cookies used on our website are from Google Analytics, as described above. We do not use cookies from advertising networks, social media platforms, or other third-party services that track your activity across multiple websites.

We do not allow third-party advertisers to place cookies on our website.

7. Your Choices and Rights

You have control over how cookies are used on your device:

Right to Accept or Reject

You can choose whether to accept cookies when you first visit our website or change your preferences at any time through your browser settings.

Right to Delete

You can delete all cookies stored on your device at any time through your browser settings.

Right to Opt-Out

You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking without affecting your ability to use our website.

Impact of Disabling Cookies

If you disable cookies:

  • Essential cookies: May prevent some website features from working properly
  • Analytics cookies: Will not affect your browsing experience, but will prevent us from understanding how to improve our website

8. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you do not want your online activity tracked. Currently, there is no universal standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals.

While we respect your privacy choices, we do not currently respond to DNT signals because:

  • There is no industry consensus on DNT implementation
  • We use minimal tracking (only Google Analytics with anonymized data)
  • You can opt out of Google Analytics using the methods described above
